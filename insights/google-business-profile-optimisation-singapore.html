The Google Business Profile Optimisation Checklist for Singapore Businesses

Your Google Business Profile is the single most important piece of digital real estate your business owns. Most Singapore businesses have set it up once and never touched it again. Here's what a properly optimised profile actually looks like.

TLDR — Key Points
  • Your Google Business Profile is the most important piece of digital real estate your business owns — most Singapore businesses have set it up once and never touched it again
  • Google Maps rankings are determined by relevance, distance, and prominence — your profile directly influences all three
  • A fully optimised profile includes: complete business information, keyword-rich description, full services list, regular photos, weekly posts, populated Q&A, and active review management
  • Most Singapore businesses have filled in roughly 40% of the available fields — doing this properly puts you ahead immediately
  • The primary business category is one of the most heavily weighted ranking signals — choosing the wrong one costs you searches every day
  • Profiles with photos get 42% more direction requests and 35% more website clicks
  • This checklist covers the foundation — full ranking dominance requires citation building, schema markup, and ongoing competitive monitoring

If someone searches for your type of business in Singapore right now, Google shows them three results before anything else. Those three businesses — the ones in what Google calls the local pack — get the overwhelming majority of clicks, calls, and walk-ins.

Your Google Business Profile is what determines whether you're one of those three businesses, or whether you're invisible. To understand why so many Singapore businesses rank #7 on Google Maps, the Google Business Profile is usually the first place to look.

The good news is that most of your competitors haven't optimised theirs either. In a market where the bar is low, doing the basics properly puts you ahead of the majority.

Section 1: The fundamentals

These are the non-negotiables. If any of these are wrong or missing, fix them before anything else.

Business name

Use your exact legal or trading name. Don't add keywords to your business name — "ABC Dental | Best Dentist Singapore" violates Google's guidelines and can get your listing suspended. Your name should be exactly what appears on your shopfront and your invoices.

Primary category

This is one of the most heavily weighted signals in the entire ranking algorithm. Choose the most specific category that accurately describes your main business. "Dental Clinic" outperforms "Health" every time. Look at what categories the top-ranked businesses in your area are using — that's your benchmark.

Secondary categories

You can add multiple categories. A dental clinic might add "Cosmetic Dentist," "Teeth Whitening Service," and "Dental Implants Provider" as secondary categories. Each one expands the range of searches you're eligible to appear for.

Address and phone number

Your address and phone number must be identical — character for character — to how they appear on your website, on Yelp, on Facebook, and anywhere else your business is listed online. Even small differences ("Pte Ltd" versus "Pte. Ltd.") create inconsistency signals that suppress your ranking.

Business hours

Keep these accurate and up to date. Include special hours for public holidays. A listing that shows "open" when you're actually closed destroys trust instantly — and Google tracks when customers report inaccurate information.

Section 2: Your business description

You have 750 characters to describe your business. Most Singapore businesses write two sentences and leave it at that. Use the full space.

Write your description the way a satisfied customer would describe you to a friend — what you do, who you do it for, what makes you worth choosing, and where you're located. Mention your key services naturally. Mention your area or neighbourhood.

What not to do: don't stuff keywords unnaturally, don't make claims you can't back up ("best dental clinic in Singapore"), and don't copy your About page word for word.

Section 3: Services and products

Google gives you a dedicated section to list every service you offer — with individual names, descriptions, and prices if applicable. Most businesses list three to five services vaguely. The businesses ranking at the top typically have 20 to 40 individual services listed, each with a short description that naturally includes the words customers search for.

For a dental clinic, this means individual listings for: general check-up and cleaning, teeth whitening, Invisalign, porcelain veneers, dental implants, wisdom tooth extraction, root canal treatment, children's dentistry, and so on. Each one is an additional signal to Google about what you do — and each one makes you eligible for more specific searches.

Section 4: Photos

Google's own data shows that businesses with photos on their profile receive 42% more requests for directions and 35% more clicks through to their website than businesses without photos.

The minimum you should have:

  • Your shopfront from the outside — so people can find you
  • Your interior — so people know what to expect
  • Your team — faces build trust faster than anything else
  • Your work or products — before and after for dental, completed projects for renovation, finished dishes for F&B
  • At least one new photo added per month going forward

Don't use stock photography. Google's algorithm deprioritises it, and patients can tell immediately.

Section 5: Google reviews

Reviews are both a ranking signal and a conversion signal. They affect where you appear and whether people choose you once they find you. Read the full guide on why Google reviews are worth more than ads — and how to get 3x more of them.

The basics: respond to every review — positive and negative. Never buy reviews or use review gating. Ask every satisfied customer with a direct link within 24–48 hours of a positive experience.

Section 6: Posts

Google Business Profile lets you publish updates directly to your listing. Posting regularly signals to Google that your business is active. Businesses that post weekly consistently outrank businesses that don't, all else being equal.

What to post: current promotions, new services, staff news, answers to common questions, photos of recent work. Each post should include a call to action. Posts expire after seven days — weekly publishing keeps your listing fresh.

Section 7: The Q&A section

Most businesses don't know this section exists. It does, and Google indexes it. You can — and should — add your own questions and answers to your profile before customers start adding theirs. Think about the five to ten questions your customers ask most often: "Do you accept walk-ins?", "Is parking available?", "Do you accept MediSave?", "What are your weekend hours?"

Answer them clearly and naturally. This content is indexed by Google and contributes to your relevance signals.

What this checklist doesn't cover

This checklist covers the foundation. A fully optimised Google Business Profile goes further — citation building across 30+ Singapore directories, schema markup on your website confirming your business details to Google, a structured approach to building review velocity, and ongoing competitive monitoring. Key Takeaways
  • Your Google Business Profile name must exactly match your shopfront and invoices — no keyword additions
  • Your primary category should be the most specific accurate description — check what top-ranked competitors use
  • Your business description has 750 characters — use them all, naturally, with key services and location mentioned
  • List every service individually with its own description — each one expands your eligible search queries
  • Upload at least 10 real photos — shopfront, interior, team, work samples — and add new ones monthly
  • Respond to every review within 24 hours — it's both a ranking signal and a trust signal
  • Publish at least one Google Business Profile post per week
  • Populate your Q&A section yourself before customers do — Google indexes this content
