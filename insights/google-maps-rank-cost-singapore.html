Why Your Google Maps Rank Is Costing You S$3,000+ a Month — And 5 Fixes You Can Do This Week

Most Singapore business owners don't know their Google Maps rank. The ones who do usually don't know what it's actually costing them. Here's how to find out — and what to do about it this week.

TLDR — Key Points
  • Your Google Maps rank is a number most Singapore business owners don't know — and it's costing them money every day
  • The top 3 Maps results capture 44% of all clicks — at rank #7 you're receiving roughly 2–3% of available traffic
  • For a business with an average transaction value of S$200+, the monthly revenue gap between rank #3 and rank #7 typically exceeds S$3,000
  • You can check your current rank right now — open Google Maps on your phone and search for your category and area

There's a number sitting quietly in the background of your business right now. You probably don't know what it is. And it's costing you money every single day.

It's your Google Maps rank for your primary keyword. Not your website ranking. Not your Google Ads position. Your rank in the local pack — the three businesses that appear on Google Maps when someone in Singapore searches for what you do.

If you're not in those top three, you're not just missing out on a few extra customers. You're effectively funding your competitors' growth with every search that goes past you.

The maths most business owners haven't done

Say 500 people search for your type of business in your area every month. At rank #3, you might be getting 60 to 80 of those clicks. At rank #7, you're getting 10 to 15.

That's 50 to 65 potential customers every month that your competitor is getting instead of you. If your average customer is worth S$600 — conservative for most Singapore service businesses — that's S$30,000 to S$39,000 in revenue going to someone else. Every month.

Even at S$200 average transaction value, the gap exceeds S$3,000 per month. The headline figure is not an exaggeration for most Singapore SMEs. Use the revenue leak calculator on our homepage to put your own numbers to it.

How to find your actual rank right now

Open Google Maps on your phone — not your computer, because your browser history and location affect desktop results. Make sure you're searching from your business area or ask someone who lives nearby to do it.

Search for the keyword your customers would actually type. Not your business name — the category. "Dentist Bedok." "Renovation contractor Singapore." "Aircon servicing Jurong West." Count down the results until you find your business. That number is your current rank.

Write it down. It's your baseline. Everything from here is about moving it up.

Why rankings change — and why yours might be slipping

Google Maps rankings are not static. They shift based on what you do, what your competitors do, and how Google's algorithm weights different signals. The most common reason Singapore businesses slip in ranking without realising it is competitor activity. A competitor gets 10 new reviews in a month. Another starts posting weekly updates. A third builds out their website with new service pages. Each of these moves the needle against you.

If you haven't actively worked on your Google Maps presence in the last three months, there's a reasonable chance your rank has slipped. The businesses at the top didn't get there by setting it up once. Read the full guide on why Singapore businesses rank #7 on Google Maps for the complete picture.

Five fixes you can do this week

Fix 01
Complete your business description
Log into your Google Business Profile at business.google.com. Find the "Description" field. You have 750 characters. Most Singapore businesses have two vague sentences. Write a proper description — what you do, who you serve, where you are, what makes you worth choosing. Use the words your customers actually search for, written naturally. This takes 20 minutes and is one of the most underutilised ranking signals on the entire platform. See the full Google Business Profile optimisation checklist for everything else in the profile that matters.
Fix 02
Add your full services list
In your Google Business Profile, find the "Services" section. Add every service you offer as a separate entry, each with its own name and a short description. A dental clinic should have individual entries for general check-up, teeth cleaning, teeth whitening, Invisalign, dental implants, wisdom tooth extraction, and so on. Each service entry is an additional signal to Google about what you do — and each one makes you eligible for more specific searches.
Fix 03
Ask three customers for a Google review today
Not next week. Today. Think of three customers you've served recently who left happy. Send each a WhatsApp message — personal, brief, genuine. Tell them you're trying to help more people find your business on Google and you'd appreciate a quick review. Include your direct Google review link so it takes 30 seconds, not five minutes. Three reviews won't transform your ranking overnight. But if you do this every week — three customers, every week — you'll have built a consistent review velocity within two months that most competitors don't have. Read the full guide on building a Google review system.
Fix 04
Add five photos to your profile today
Log back into your Google Business Profile. Go to "Photos." Upload at least five photos today — your shopfront from outside, your interior, your team or yourself at work, and examples of your work or products. Google's data shows businesses with photos receive 42% more requests for directions and 35% more website clicks than those without. Use your phone. The photos don't need to be professionally shot — they just need to be real, current, and clear.
Fix 05
Publish a Google Business Profile post today
From your Google Business Profile dashboard, find the "Posts" section and write an update. It can be a current promotion, a service highlight, a recent piece of work, or a brief update. Keep it short — two to three sentences and a photo. Include a call to action at the end: "Call us to book" or "Visit our website." Publish it today. Then set a reminder to do it again next week. Consistent posting is a direct signal to Google that your business is active — and active businesses rank above dormant ones.

What these fixes won't do

These five things will help. They won't be enough on their own to take you from rank #7 to rank #1 in a competitive Singapore category. The complete picture — consistent citation building across 30+ directories, a structured review acquisition system, service and area pages built out on your website, schema markup, competitive monitoring — takes more time and a more structured approach. But these five fixes are the right starting point. They cost nothing but time, they can be done this week, and they'll start showing Google that your business is worth paying attention to.
