If you've started looking into local SEO for your Singapore business, you've probably noticed that nobody seems to want to publish their prices. Agency websites say "contact us for a custom quote." Freelancers on Fiverr charge S$50 for things that sound impressive but do very little. This guide names numbers. It explains what different price points actually buy you, where the traps are, and how to figure out what's right for your business before you spend anything.

Why local SEO pricing varies so much

Local SEO is not one thing. It's a collection of activities — optimising your Google Business Profile, building citations, generating reviews, creating content pages, building backlinks — and different providers do different combinations of these at different levels of quality.

A freelancer charging S$300 a month might be doing one or two of these things superficially. An agency charging S$8,000 a month might be doing all of them deeply, with a team of specialists, reporting infrastructure, and ongoing testing. The wide price range reflects genuine differences in scope, depth, and consistency — not just margin.

The other factor is competition. Ranking a renovation contractor in Jurong takes less effort than ranking a dental clinic in Orchard. More competitive categories require more work to move and more work to hold.

The freelancer vs agency question

Freelancers work well when the scope is narrow and well-defined — "set up my Google Business Profile properly" or "build citations across 30 directories." These are finite tasks with a clear output.

Freelancers struggle with ongoing execution. Local SEO requires weekly posts, monthly content, regular monitoring, and adaptation when competitors move. Most freelancers are managing multiple clients and don't have the bandwidth to be proactive consistently.

The other consideration is accountability. If your ranking doesn't move, an agency has a team you can escalate to and a reputation to protect. A freelancer can simply stop responding.

What to watch out for

Guaranteed rankings

Nobody can guarantee a specific Google Maps rank. Google's algorithm changes constantly and is influenced by factors outside any provider's control. Any provider guaranteeing "top 3 in 30 days" is either misleading you or planning to use tactics that work short-term and damage your listing long-term.

Long contracts with no performance clause

A 12-month contract is reasonable in local SEO because meaningful results take time. But that contract should include a performance clause — a clear definition of what "working" looks like and an exit option if it isn't happening. Vanguardeer's contracts include a 90-day performance clause: if your ranking hasn't moved measurably by Day 90, you can exit with 30 days notice and no penalty.

Vague reporting

Your monthly report should show your ranking for specific keywords, before and after. If your provider is reporting on "impressions" and "engagement" without showing you actual rank movement, ask why.
